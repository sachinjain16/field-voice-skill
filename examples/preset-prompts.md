# Field Voice preset prompts

Use these prompts when you want the skill to apply a complete settings bundle without spelling out every parameter.

## Client-safe

Best for customer-ready email or message cleanup.

```text
/field-voice

Preset: Client-safe

[paste draft]
```

## Decision ask

Best when the customer needs to make a decision, confirm a path, or commit to a next step.

```text
/field-voice

Preset: Decision ask

Make the ask clear, but keep the tone collaborative.

[paste draft]
```

## Customer follow-up

Best after a customer or partner meeting.

```text
/field-voice

Preset: Customer follow-up

Turn this into a customer-ready recap. Preserve only decisions, open questions, and next steps that are explicitly in the notes.

[paste meeting notes]
```

## Executive brief

Best for leadership updates, ROB notes, forecast summaries, and business reviews.

```text
/field-voice

Preset: Executive brief

Turn this into a crisp update with status, signal, risk, ask, and next step. Do not add facts.

[paste notes]
```

## Manager update

Best for weekly updates, blocker notes, and escalation framing.

```text
/field-voice

Preset: Manager update

Make this direct and manager-ready. Pull out progress, risk, ask, and next step.

[paste draft]
```

## Technical credible

Best for architecture, migration, AI, security, data, and platform explanations.

```text
/field-voice

Preset: Technical credible

Make this clearer and more credible. Preserve exact product names, technical terms, versions, citations, and qualifiers.

[paste technical text]
```

## LinkedIn human

Best for professional public posts.

```text
/field-voice

Preset: LinkedIn human

Make this sound like a real person. Keep it professional, reflective, and non-cringey. Remove corporate hype and engagement bait.

[paste draft]
```

## Quick reply

Best for short internal chat responses.

```text
/field-voice

Preset: Quick reply

Keep this under three lines.

[paste reply]
```

## Deck sharpen

Best for slide titles, bullets, or talk tracks.

```text
/field-voice

Preset: Deck sharpen

Tighten this for an executive slide. Keep the point, reduce words, and preserve the slide intent.

[paste slide text]
```

## Preset plus personal profile

Best when the user wants the preset safety behavior but still wants the output to sound like them.

```text
/field-voice

Profile: [paste profile or profile name]
Preset: Client-safe

Make it a little warmer, but do not add new facts or commitments.

[paste draft]
```
