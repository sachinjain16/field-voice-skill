Mode-based rewriting

Field Voice adapts the rewrite to the channel. A LinkedIn post should not sound like an executive brief. A chat reply should not sound like a memo.

Customer email

Warm, clear, professional, with a specific ask. Avoids internal-only reasoning.

Executive brief

Crisp, outcome-first, no fluff. Useful for status, signal, risk, ask, and next step.

Chat reply

Short, natural, conversational, and easy to send in chat.

LinkedIn post

Human, reflective, credible, and non-cringey. No corporate hype or engagement bait.

Deck copy

Tight, punchy, executive-readable slide titles, bullets, and talk tracks.

Technical explanation

Precise, plainspoken, jargon-aware, and low hype.

Source-Locked Credibility Mode

The V2 anchor feature: make the writing sound more human without making it less true. This mode is built for customer-facing and technical content where extra AI detail creates credibility risk.

Source-locked rewrite

Rewrites only what is present. No new facts, dates, metrics, names, owners, claims, citations, or commitments.

Citation handling

Preserves provided citations, avoids new citations, and flags claims that need a source.

Commitment check

Flags timeline, pricing, availability, funding, support, approval, and delivery commitments.

Safety posture

Field Voice drafts only. It does not send, post, publish, reply, forward, or update content without explicit user approval. It preserves customer confidentiality and avoids adding unsupported claims.

No invented proof

No fake metrics, dates, sources, quotes, or customer details.

No oversharing

Private calendar, email, chat, or internal rationale stays private.

No over-humanizing

Professional credibility beats slang, fake vulnerability, and performative personality.
